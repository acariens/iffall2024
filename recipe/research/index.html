Jamaican Jerk Chicken

Description

Jerk chicken is a spicy, flavorful dish that originated in Jamaica and is popular throughout the Caribbean. It's made by coating meat in a marinade of spices and slow-cooking it over a fire or grill, which gives it a smoky flavor. The main ingredients in the marinade are allspice and Scotch bonnet peppers.


Ingredients

  1. 1 medium onion, coarsely chopped
  2. 3 medium scallions, chopped
  3. 2 Scotch bonnet chiles, chopped
  4. 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  5. 1 tablespoon five-spice powder
  6. 1 tablespoon allspice berries, coarsely ground
  7. 1 tablespoon coarsely ground black pepper
  8. 1 teaspoon dried thyme, crumbled
  9. 1 teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg
  10. 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  11. 1/2 cup soy sauce
  12. 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  13. 2 (3 1/2 to 4-pound) chickens, quartered

Instructions

Step 1

In a food processor, combine the onion, scallions, chiles, garlic, five-spice powder, allspice, pepper, thyme, nutmeg, and salt; process to a coarse paste.


Step 2

With the machine on, add the soy sauce and oil in a steady stream.


Step 3

Pour the marinade into a large, shallow dish, add the chicken, and turn to coat.


Step 4

Cover and refrigerate overnight. Bring the chicken to room temperature before proceeding.


Step 5

Light a grill. Grill the chicken over a medium-hot fire, turning occasionally, until well browned and cooked through, 35 to 40 minutes. (Cover the grill for a smokier flavor.)


Step 6

Transfer the chicken to a platter and serve.

NYT Cooking

I have always thought of NYT Cooking as a good recipe website because of it's emphasis on browsing. With an open interface and scrollable options, it encourages users to come to the website without a plan and peruse until they see something they like. Appealing photos, easy to read instructions, and consistency in recipe quality all compliment the intuitive menu bar interface.


Green Kitchen Stories

In contrast to many other recipe websites, Green Kitchen Stories is quite minimalist. The entire homepage and interface is very linear. And, the recipe specific page is just one long scrollable page with filter and search options. While I think NYT Cooking focuses on categories to initially split up information, Green Kitchen Stories presents everything in a simple way and lets users decide how they want to narrow their search.


Downshiftology

Downshiftology's recipe page has the option to filter by diet, ingredient, and method. All presented with professional photographs, I just liked the focus on inclusive eating in the search area which adds to an already minimalist and inviting website.
